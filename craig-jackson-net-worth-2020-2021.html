Career

Craig Jackson is the president of Barrett-Jackson Collector Car Auction, which was founded in 1971 by his father Ross and Tom Barrett. Initially, the auction was known as the “Fiesta del Auto Elegance.” This auction was originally meant to raise funds for local charities. Initially, the auction didn’t involve cars until 1972, and the first car the auctioned was Adolf Hitler’s armored Mercedes-Benz 770K. This was bought for over 150 000 USD, which was a lot of money in the early 70s.

Craig started working with his father at a very young age. He worked under his father until 1997 when he took over operations entirely. One of his achievements over the years is the addition of online bidding as well as auction airs on SPEED channel. Craig is praised for professionalism, incredible attention to detail, and excellent personal car collection.

Craig Jackson’s car collection is housed in what is acknowledged as one of the most luxurious and expensive garages in the world.
