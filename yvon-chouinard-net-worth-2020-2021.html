Early Life & Biography

Yvon Chouinard was born on 9th November 1938 in Lewiston, Maine, the U.S., His father, worked as a mechanic, handyman and a plumber from a French-Canadian background. He spent most of his childhood in Southern California after his family relocated from Maine when he was eight years old.

He spent most of his early young adult life rock climbing. His interest in conserving the environment started when he was still young. He became a member of Sierra Club, which was focused on the environment. Yvon, still in his youth, founded Southern California Falconry Club. His love for rock climbing led him to start making his climbing gear.

Career

Chouinard’s career was inspired by his love of outdoors, more so rock climbing. He began crafting his climbing tools and eventually learning how to be a blacksmith.  He later established his clothing line in 1970, known as Patagonia. Before the company’s conception, he would buy rugby t-shirts while on a trip to Scotland and later making a profit out of selling them.  The company started growing to even make their rugged t-shirts.  The company later evolved to practicing environment conservation.

The company’s revenue started increasing in the mid-80s through to 1990 from 20 million dollars to over 100 million dollars. Chouinard resorted to donating most of his income to environmental conservation programs.  What’s more fascinating about his is the fact that he encourages his employees to conserve the environment and even tithe to such causes. His company mainly uses environmentally friendly products. When it comes to manufacturing of the clothing line, they use 100% organic cotton.

The business mogul does not fancy making money as much as taking care of the environment. Many investors have declared interest in buying shares in his company, Patagonia, to no luck. He expressed no interest throughout the years to selling his company no matter how much money he was being offered. To date, he still owns 100% of his company. Patagonia currently boasts of revenues worth $800 million and counting.  Furthermore, he also founded Black Diamond Equipment ltd a manufacturing company for climbing equipment.
